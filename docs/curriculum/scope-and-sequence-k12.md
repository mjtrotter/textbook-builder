# Complete K-12 Scope & Sequence

Detailed year-by-year content progression for all content and skill strands.

---

## How to Read This Document

Each grade level shows:
- **Content**: What students learn (knowledge)
- **Skills**: How students think (transferable)
- **Integration**: Cross-curricular connections
- **Assessments**: How mastery is demonstrated

**Note**: Reading is NOT listed as a subject. Reading skills are developed through the content in History, Science, and Bible.

---

## MATHEMATICS

### Kindergarten
**Content Focus**: Number sense, counting, shapes
- Count to 100 by ones and tens
- Write numbers 0-20
- Compare quantities (more, less, equal)
- Identify and describe 2D and 3D shapes
- Sort objects by attributes
- Addition/subtraction within 5 (concrete)

**Logic Integration**: Sorting, classifying, same/different

**Key Assessments**: Oral counting, number writing, shape identification

---

### Grade 1
**Content Focus**: Addition/subtraction facts, place value
- Add/subtract within 20 (fluency within 10)
- Understand place value (tens and ones)
- Measure lengths indirectly
- Tell time to hour and half-hour
- Identify coins by name and value
- Organize and interpret data

**Logic Integration**: If-then statements, pattern completion

**Key Assessments**: Math fact fluency, place value demonstrations

---

### Grade 2
**Content Focus**: Multi-digit operations, measurement
- Add/subtract within 1000
- Understand even/odd numbers
- Measure and estimate lengths
- Tell time to 5 minutes
- Count money and make change
- Introduction to multiplication (equal groups)

**Logic Integration**: Analogies, sequence patterns

**Key Assessments**: Two-digit addition/subtraction, word problems

---

### Grade 3
**Content Focus**: Multiplication/division, fractions
- Multiply/divide within 100 (fluency)
- Understand fractions as numbers
- Equivalent fractions
- Area and perimeter
- Scaled graphs and pictographs
- Round to nearest 10 or 100

**Logic Integration**: Pattern generalization, prediction

**Competition Option**: MOEMS preparation begins

**Key Assessments**: Multiplication facts, fraction concepts

---

### Grade 4
**Content Focus**: Multi-digit operations, fraction operations
- Multi-digit multiplication (4-digit × 1-digit)
- Long division with remainders
- Add/subtract fractions (like denominators)
- Decimal notation for fractions
- Angle measurement
- Factors and multiples

**Logic Integration**: Introduction to formal logic (categorical statements)

**Competition Option**: MOEMS Division E

**Key Assessments**: Multi-digit operations, fraction addition/subtraction

---

### Grade 5
**Content Focus**: Fraction operations, decimals, coordinate plane
- Multiply/divide fractions
- All decimal operations
- Coordinate plane graphing
- Volume of rectangular prisms
- Order of operations
- Introduction to expressions

**Logic Integration**: Categorical syllogisms, valid/invalid arguments

**Competition Option**: MOEMS Division M, pre-MATHCOUNTS

**Key Assessments**: Fraction operations, decimal operations, expressions

---

### Grade 6
**Content Focus**: Ratios, integers, pre-algebra
- Ratios and proportional relationships
- Operations with negative numbers
- Expressions and equations
- Inequalities
- Statistical measures
- Surface area and volume

**Logic Integration**: Deductive reasoning, basic proof structures

**Competition Option**: MATHCOUNTS Chapter, AMC 8

**Key Assessments**: Ratio problems, integer operations, equation solving

---

### Grade 7
**Content Focus**: Algebra I (first half for core, full for accelerated)
- Linear equations and inequalities
- Proportional relationships and graphing
- Geometry: angle relationships, triangles
- Probability and sampling
- Algebraic expressions and operations

**Logic Integration**: Conditional statements, converses, contrapositives

**Competition Option**: MATHCOUNTS State, AMC 8

**Key Assessments**: Equation solving, graphing, geometric reasoning

---

### Grade 8
**Content Focus**: Algebra I completion / Geometry start
- Functions and linear models
- Systems of linear equations
- Exponents and scientific notation
- Pythagorean theorem
- Transformations
- Irrational numbers

**Logic Integration**: Introduction to geometric proof

**Competition Option**: AMC 8, AMC 10 prep

**Key Assessments**: Functions, systems, Pythagorean applications

---

### Grade 9
**Content Focus**: Geometry (or Algebra II for accelerated)
- Congruence and similarity
- Coordinate geometry proofs
- Right triangle trigonometry
- Circles
- Area and volume
- Formal two-column proofs

**Logic Integration**: Formal geometric proof throughout

**Competition Option**: AMC 10

**Key Assessments**: Proofs, trigonometry, coordinate geometry

---

### Grade 10
**Content Focus**: Algebra II (or Pre-Calculus for accelerated)
- Polynomial functions
- Rational functions
- Exponential and logarithmic functions
- Sequences and series
- Conic sections
- Matrices

**Logic Integration**: Logical equivalence, proof by contradiction

**Competition Option**: AMC 10/12

**Key Assessments**: Function analysis, complex problem solving

---

### Grade 11
**Content Focus**: Pre-Calculus (or Calculus for accelerated)
- Trigonometric functions and identities
- Polar coordinates
- Vectors
- Limits (conceptual)
- Parametric equations
- Probability and combinatorics

**Logic Integration**: Mathematical induction

**Competition Option**: AMC 12, AIME prep

**Key Assessments**: Trigonometry, advanced function analysis

---

### Grade 12
**Content Focus**: Calculus and/or Statistics
- Limits and continuity
- Derivatives and applications
- Integrals and applications
- OR: Statistical inference, regression, probability

**Logic Integration**: Epsilon-delta proofs (Calculus)

**Competition Option**: AIME, USAMO prep

**Key Assessments**: AP Calculus or AP Statistics exams

## HISTORY & GEOGRAPHY

### Four-Year Cycle Overview

| Cycle | Period | Key Focus |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Year 1 | Ancient (Creation-476 AD) | Foundations of civilization |
| Year 2 | Medieval (476-1600) | Christendom, Islam, exploration |
| Year 3 | Early Modern (1600-1850) | Revolutions, founding |
| Year 4 | Modern (1850-Present) | Ideologies, world wars |

---

### GRAMMAR STAGE (Grades K-4): Narrative Focus

#### Kindergarten (Ancient)
- Creation narrative
- Noah and the flood
- Ancient Egypt: pyramids, Nile, pharaohs
- Ancient Israel: patriarchs, Moses, David
- Introduction to maps and globes

**Reading Through History**: Picture books, read-alouds of Bible stories and Egyptian tales

---

#### Grade 1 (Medieval)
- Fall of Rome
- Vikings and Norsemen
- Knights and castles
- Medieval church and monasteries
- Marco Polo and exploration

**Reading Through History**: Knight tales, saint biographies, adventure narratives

---

#### Grade 2 (Early Modern)
- Age of Exploration: Columbus, Magellan
- Reformation: Luther, Calvin
- Colonial America: Jamestown, Plymouth
- American Revolution basics
- Constitution and founding

**Reading Through History**: Explorer biographies, colonial life narratives

---

#### Grade 3 (Modern)
- Westward expansion
- Civil War (narrative focus)
- Immigration and industrialization
- World Wars (narrative focus)
- Civil Rights movement

**Reading Through History**: Pioneer stories, immigrant narratives, war-time stories

---

#### Grade 4 (Ancient - Cycle 2)
- Near Eastern civilizations: Mesopotamia, Persia
- Ancient Greece: city-states, philosophy, wars
- Ancient Rome: Republic to Empire
- Early Church: Apostles, persecutions
- Geography: ancient world regions

**Reading Through History**: Greek myths (critically), Roman narratives, Church history

---

### LOGIC STAGE (Grades 5-8): Analysis Focus

#### Grade 5 (Medieval - Cycle 2)
- Byzantine Empire
- Rise of Islam
- Feudal system analysis
- Crusades: causes and effects
- Renaissance: art, humanism
- Reformation: theological analysis

**Primary Sources**: Magna Carta excerpts, Luther's 95 Theses
**Writing**: Compare/contrast essays, cause-effect analysis

---

#### Grade 6 (Early Modern - Cycle 2)
- Scientific Revolution
- Enlightenment philosophy
- American Revolution: documents and debates
- French Revolution: causes and terror
- Industrial Revolution: social effects
- American expansion and slavery

**Primary Sources**: Declaration of Independence, Federalist Papers, slave narratives
**Writing**: Document-based questions, argumentative essays

---

#### Grade 7 (Modern - Cycle 2)
- Civil War: causes, battles, reconstruction
- Imperialism and colonialism
- World War I: causes and effects
- Russian Revolution
- Great Depression
- World War II and Holocaust

**Primary Sources**: Lincoln's speeches, WWI letters, Holocaust testimonies
**Writing**: Research papers, perspective analysis

---

#### Grade 8 (Ancient - Cycle 3)
- Ancient worldviews: polytheism vs. monotheism
- Greek philosophy: pre-Socratics to Aristotle
- Roman law and governance
- Jewish thought and Second Temple period
- Early Church theology

**Primary Sources**: Excerpts from philosophers, Roman laws, Church fathers
**Writing**: Philosophical analysis, historiographical introduction

---

### RHETORIC STAGE (Grades 9-12): College Level

#### Grade 9 (Medieval - Cycle 3)
- Medieval philosophy: Augustine to Aquinas
- Islamic Golden Age
- Byzantine theology
- Scholasticism
- Reformation theology in depth

**Primary Sources**: Confessions, Summa excerpts, Institutes
**Writing**: Theological argumentation, original thesis

---

#### Grade 10 (Early Modern - Cycle 3)
- Enlightenment political philosophy
- American founding: intellectual roots
- Conservative vs. liberal traditions
- Economic theory: Smith to Marx
- Nationalism and revolution

**Primary Sources**: Locke, Montesquieu, Burke, Paine, Marx
**Writing**: Political philosophy papers, comparative analysis

---

#### Grade 11 (Modern - Cycle 3)
- Ideologies: liberalism, conservatism, socialism, fascism
- Total war and genocide
- Cold War: ideological conflict
- Postmodernism and its critics
- Contemporary issues

**Primary Sources**: 20th century speeches, manifestos, critiques
**Writing**: Extended research papers, historiographical essays

---

#### Grade 12: Electives
Options:
- AP US History
- AP European History
- AP World History
- Government and Economics
- Philosophy seminar

## LANGUAGE ARTS (Grammar, Writing, Speaking)

**Remember**: Reading is not included here—it happens through History, Science, and Bible.

### Grammar Stage (K-4)

#### Kindergarten
**Grammar**: Oral language development, letter sounds
**Writing**: Name writing, copywork (letters, then words)
**Speaking**: Show and tell, recitation (short poems, verses)
**Latin**: Not yet

---

#### Grade 1
**Grammar**: Phonics completion, parts of speech (noun, verb)
**Writing**: Copywork (sentences), oral narration
**Speaking**: Longer recitations, story retelling
**Latin**: Not yet

---

#### Grade 2
**Grammar**: Complete sentences, capitalization, punctuation, adjectives
**Writing**: Copywork, dictation begins, written narration (1-2 sentences)
**Speaking**: Oral reports, longer recitations
**Latin**: Latin roots vocabulary (10/week)

---

#### Grade 3
**Grammar**: Pronouns, adverbs, sentence diagramming begins
**Writing**: Dictation, narration (paragraph), summarization
**Speaking**: Book reports, presentation skills
**Latin**: Latin roots (15/week), simple phrases

---

#### Grade 4
**Grammar**: Complete sentence diagramming, verb tenses, complex sentences
**Writing**: Narration, basic composition (introduction, body, conclusion)
**Speaking**: Prepared speeches, dramatic reading
**Latin**: Latin Grammar I begins (First Form Latin or similar)

---

### Logic Stage (5-8)

#### Grade 5
**Grammar**: Advanced diagramming, verbals, clauses
**Writing**: Five-paragraph essay, paraphrase, précis
**Speaking**: Expository speeches, recitation competition
**Latin**: Latin Grammar II

---

#### Grade 6
**Grammar**: Usage and style, agreement, parallelism
**Writing**: Research paper introduction, argumentation basics
**Speaking**: Persuasive speeches, debate introduction
**Latin**: Latin Grammar III

---

#### Grade 7
**Grammar**: Rhetoric introduction, style analysis
**Writing**: Thesis papers, counterargument, MLA format
**Speaking**: Formal debate, Socratic seminar leadership
**Latin**: Latin reading (adapted texts)

---

#### Grade 8
**Grammar**: Advanced rhetoric, logical fallacies in writing
**Writing**: Extended research, multiple sources, literary analysis
**Speaking**: Original speeches, debate competition
**Latin**: Latin literature (simplified)

---

### Rhetoric Stage (9-12)

#### Grade 9
**Grammar**: Style refinement, voice development
**Writing**: Literary analysis, research methodology
**Speaking**: Original orations, apologetics
**Latin**: Classical Latin literature

---

#### Grade 10
**Grammar**: Advanced style (imitation of masters)
**Writing**: Extended literary analysis, philosophy papers
**Speaking**: Public speaking, thesis defense practice
**Latin**: Caesar, Cicero (selections)

---

#### Grade 11
**Grammar**: Professional writing conventions
**Writing**: College-level research papers, original argumentation
**Speaking**: Formal thesis defense, mock trial
**Latin**: Virgil, advanced authors

---

#### Grade 12
**Grammar**: Polish and refine
**Writing**: Senior thesis project
**Speaking**: Senior oration
**Latin**: Completion and review

## SKILL PROGRESSION SUMMARY

| Skill | K-2 | 3-4 | 5-6 | 7-8 | 9-12 |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|------|
| **Reasoning** | If-then, patterns | Analogies, sequences | Formal logic | Applied logic, proof | Philosophical reasoning |
| **Writing** | Copywork, oral narration | Dictation, paragraphs | Essays, research intro | Thesis papers | College-level |
| **Oratory** | Recitation | Speeches, reports | Debate, Socratic | Competition, defense | Senior oration |
| **Reading** | Decode, fluency | Comprehension | Analysis | Critical reading | Scholarly |
| **Discovery** | Observe, question | Hypothesize, test | Design experiments | Research methods | Original inquiry |

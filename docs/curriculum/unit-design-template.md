## SAMPLE: Grade 3 Ancient Egypt Unit

```yaml
unit:
  title: "Gift of the Nile: Ancient Egypt"
  grade: 3
  duration: "6 weeks"
  stage: "grammar"
  primary_strand: "history"
  content_focus: "Egyptian civilization, Nile River, pyramids"
  history_cycle: "ancient"
```

### Content Objectives
1. Describe daily life in ancient Egypt
2. Explain the importance of the Nile River
3. Identify key features of Egyptian religion and compare to biblical truth
4. Describe how pyramids were built

### Skill Objectives
**Primary Skill**: Analysis (comparing and contrasting)
- Compare Egyptian daily life to student's own life
- Identify similarities and differences between Egyptian religion and Christianity

**Secondary Skills**:
- Visualization: Interpret maps of ancient Egypt and the Nile
- Communication: Write clear narrations of Egyptian topics

### Cross-Curricular Integration

| Strand | Connection | Activities |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Science | Nile floods, erosion, soil | Erosion experiments, farming simulation |
| Math | Egyptian numerals, pyramid geometry | Base-10 comparison, 3D shapes |
| Grammar | Narration, descriptive writing | Narrate Egyptian daily life, descriptive pyramid paragraph |
| Bible | Joseph in Egypt, Moses, plagues | Compare Egyptian gods to true God, Joseph narrative |

### Reading Integration
- *Pharaohs of Ancient Egypt* (Landmark book) - chapters 1-4
- *Mara, Daughter of the Nile* (historical fiction) - selected chapters
- Exodus 1-12 (Bible)

### Differentiation
**Support**: Picture-heavy resources, pre-taught vocabulary (pharaoh, papyrus, hieroglyphics), simplified Landmark book sections
**Enrichment**: Research specific pharaoh, build pyramid model to scale, compare Egyptian math to our system

### Biblical Integration
**Worldview**: Egyptians had many gods; the true God showed His power over all of them through the plagues
**Scripture**: Exodus 7-12 (plagues), Genesis 41-50 (Joseph)
**Virtue**: Trust in God (Joseph trusted God in Egypt)

### Culminating Project
**Narrate and Illustrate**: Create illustrated narration of "A Day in the Life of an Egyptian Child" demonstrating knowledge of daily life, geography, and religion, with comparison to student's own life and biblical worldview.

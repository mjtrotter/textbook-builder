# Discovery and Scientific Method Curriculum

A K-12 progression developing inquiry skills, observational acuity, and systematic investigation across all subjects.

---

## Philosophy

### Why Discovery?

1. **Active learning**: Students learn deeply through investigation, not passive reception
2. **Transferable skill**: Inquiry and experimentation apply across all domains
3. **Wonder cultivation**: Preserving and developing natural curiosity
4. **Truth-seeking**: Systematic methods for finding and verifying truth
5. **Preparation**: Foundation for research in any field

### What is Discovery?

Discovery is the skill of systematic inquiry:
- **Observation**: Careful, detailed noticing
- **Questioning**: Generating productive questions
- **Hypothesizing**: Forming testable predictions
- **Investigating**: Designing and conducting experiments
- **Analyzing**: Interpreting evidence
- **Concluding**: Drawing warranted conclusions
- **Communicating**: Sharing findings clearly

### Beyond Science Class

Discovery is NOT limited to science. It includes:
- **Historical inquiry**: Analyzing sources, testing historical claims
- **Literary analysis**: Observing patterns, testing interpretations
- **Mathematical investigation**: Exploring conjectures, finding patterns
- **Theological inquiry**: Examining Scripture, testing interpretations
- **Linguistic discovery**: Finding patterns in language

## Grades K-2: Wonder and Noticing

### Purpose
- Preserve and cultivate natural curiosity
- Develop careful observation skills
- Begin systematic recording

### Kindergarten

**Observation Skills**:
- Use all five senses
- Describe with specific words
- Draw observations
- Compare similarities and differences

**Wonder Questions**:
- "I wonder why..."
- "I wonder what would happen if..."
- "I wonder how..."

**Recording**:
- Simple drawings
- Teacher-scribed observations
- Class charts

**Daily Practices**:
- Nature observation (outdoor time)
- Calendar and weather observation
- "I notice... I wonder..." discussions
- Science table exploration

**Sample Activities**:
- Observe seeds sprouting
- Compare leaves
- Explore magnets
- Watch ice melt
- Sort objects by properties

### Grade 1

**Observation Development**:
- Quantitative descriptions (how many, how big)
- Observation over time
- Using tools (magnifiers, rulers)
- Detailed drawings with labels

**Question Development**:
- Distinguish observations from inferences
- Generate multiple questions from one observation
- Identify testable questions

**Simple Investigations**:
- "What will happen if...?"
- Change one thing, observe result
- Record with pictures and words

**Recording**:
- Observation journals
- Simple data tables
- Before/after comparisons

**Sample Investigations**:
- What do plants need to grow?
- Which materials sink/float?
- How do shadows change through the day?
- What do worms prefer: light or dark?

### Grade 2

**Observation Refinement**:
- Multiple observations before conclusions
- Systematic comparison
- Pattern identification
- Measurement with standard units

**Fair Test Introduction**:
- Keep everything the same except one thing
- Predict outcomes
- Compare results to predictions

**Recording and Reporting**:
- Structured observation logs
- Simple graphs (pictographs, bar)
- Oral reports of investigations

**Sample Investigations**:
- Which paper airplane design flies farthest?
- Do mealworms prefer wet or dry environments?
- What affects how fast ice melts?
- How does the moon change over a month?

## Grades 3-4: Systematic Investigation

### Purpose
- Develop formal investigation skills
- Learn scientific method structure
- Apply across subjects

### Grade 3

**Scientific Method Introduction**:
1. Observe and wonder
2. Form a question
3. Research background
4. Make a hypothesis
5. Design experiment
6. Conduct experiment
7. Analyze data
8. Draw conclusion
9. Communicate findings

**Variables Concept**:
- Independent variable (what you change)
- Dependent variable (what you measure)
- Controlled variables (what stays the same)

**Investigation Skills**:
- Multiple trials
- Measurement accuracy
- Data tables
- Simple graphs (bar, line)
- Identifying patterns

**Cross-Curricular Application**:
- **History**: Compare sources on same event
- **Math**: Find patterns in number sequences
- **Literature**: Gather evidence for character analysis
- **Bible**: Compare passages on same topic

**Sample Investigations**:
- What affects the rate of evaporation?
- Do all brands of batteries last equally long?
- What is the best environment for mold growth?
- How does music affect plant growth?

### Grade 4

**Method Refinement**:
- Hypothesis as "If... then... because..."
- Operational definitions
- Precise procedures (replicable)
- Quantitative data emphasis

**Analysis Skills**:
- Mean, median, mode
- Percent change
- Graphing with scales
- Identifying trends

**Error and Uncertainty**:
- Sources of error
- Ways to improve accuracy
- Variability in data
- Unexpected results

**Science Fair Project**:
- Complete scientific method
- Display board
- Oral presentation
- Respond to questions

**Cross-Curricular Investigations**:
- **History**: Test a historical claim with evidence
- **Math**: Investigate mathematical conjectures
- **Literature**: Analyze author's patterns across works
- **Grammar**: Investigate language patterns

## Cross-Curricular Applications

### History as Discovery

**Methods**:
- Source analysis (primary and secondary)
- Corroboration
- Contextualization
- Close reading

**Investigation Questions**:
- What happened? (narrative)
- Why did it happen? (causation)
- How do we know? (evidence)
- What does it mean? (interpretation)

**Elementary**: Compare two accounts of same event
**Middle**: Analyze primary sources on historical question
**High**: Historiographical research paper

### Literature as Discovery

**Methods**:
- Close reading
- Pattern analysis
- Textual evidence
- Interpretive hypothesis testing

**Investigation Questions**:
- What patterns exist in the text?
- What does the author seem to be saying?
- How does evidence support interpretation?
- What are alternative readings?

**Elementary**: Notice and collect examples of literary elements
**Middle**: Gather evidence for character/theme analysis
**High**: Critical literary analysis with theoretical lens

### Mathematics as Discovery

**Methods**:
- Pattern recognition
- Conjecture formation
- Example/counterexample testing
- Proof

**Investigation Questions**:
- What pattern do I see?
- Is this always true?
- Why does it work?
- Can I prove it?

**Elementary**: Find patterns in number sequences
**Middle**: Investigate mathematical conjectures
**High**: Develop proofs and generalizations

### Theology as Discovery

**Methods**:
- Careful reading of Scripture
- Context analysis (historical, literary)
- Comparing passages
- Testing interpretations

**Investigation Questions**:
- What does the text say?
- What did it mean to original audience?
- How does it connect to other Scripture?
- How does it apply today?

**Elementary**: Compare different Bible stories on same theme
**Middle**: Analyze passages on theological topic
**High**: Hermeneutical and apologetics research
